San Diego State University: IT Divison, ServiceNow Deployment

Student Developer / Application Administrator

Feb 2020 - Aug 2020

In the 2020 Spring Semester I began working with San Diego State University's IT User Services Department, as a part of the ServiceNow Deployment Team. I worked with an Agile team as both an Application Administrator and Platform Developer. My core responsibilities on the team was to support IT division staff with the utilization and development of the ServiceNow platform for campus use.

Once I had demonstrated a solid understanding of the ServiceNow platform, I became one of the main contributors to the SDSU Service Catalog project. Our mission was to migrate the IT User Services catalog, which was previously listed on Azure or hosted independently by their respective departments, to a centralized university service portal. Additionally, I became responsible for the design and development of an IT Service Management mobile application built using ServiceNow's application development toolkit.

After 6 months of working with IT User Services and reaching the end of my final semester at SDSU, my employment with San Diego State University ended as the student developer position is only available to active students.

In August of 2020, I became a certified Systems Administrator after passing the ServiceNow Systems Administrator Certification exam.

Responsibilities

  • As an Application Administrator:

    I addressed IT user issues and concerns regarding the platform. Through the use of ServiceNow's Incident Management system, I supported IT process users by maintaining timely communication with the involved members, developing and testing platform solutions in the development instance, and then implementing finalized solutions into the production instance. Often times my tasks involved troubleshooting issues submitted by users, implementing new functions and features of the platform, and updating forms and user interfaces on the web portal.

  • As a Student Developer:

    I was my responsibile for being familiar with core ServiceNow platform functions and basics as well as have an understanding of the web-client's structure and scripts. The development of features and functions, including the service catalog and web portal, required knowledge of the platform's data table interactions, XML document formatting, and ServiceNow's client-side JavaScript Glide API.

    • SDSU Service Catalog/Web Portal Development

      In March of 2020, I began working with the development team to build a new service catalog for SDSU. Our task was to migrate the university's service catalog from the previous Azure implementation, to a new centralized web portal using ServiceNow. On the team I was responsibile for building, testing and implementing catalog items. This process required gathering requirements from department leadership, building workflows and task tables, constructing variable sets and catalog items, and building automated-test suites to ensure catalog integrity with every item implementation.

    • SDSU ITSM Mobile Agent iOS/Android App

      In April of 2020, after going through the initial stages of the Service Catalog implementation, I was asked to look into building a proof-of-concept ITSM Incident Management mobile application. I studied the ServiceNow Studio application builder, mobile development best-practices and UX design. The application was designed to be intuitive and provide IT process users with a clear and quick way of creating, updating and resolving incidents. Based on my experiences working in the Instructional Technology Services, I wanted to build the kind of mobile application I wish I had while I worked in the field troubleshooting issues, writing updates and communicating with ITS staff.
