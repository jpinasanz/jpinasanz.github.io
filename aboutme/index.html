A bit about myself...

My name is Juan Pina-Sanz, although I frequently go by J.C.!

I have recently graduated from San Diego State University (SDSU) where I majored in Computer Science.

Born in Venezuela, but raised in San Diego California, I began my academics wanting to study film production. Although an interest in film remained, I became quickly drawn to computer science and programming a year into my academics.

Since then I have studied mathematics earning my Associate's Degree in applied Math and my Bachelors in computer science. I spent many years working retail at my local Best Buy where I did everything from computer sales, Geek Squad, and finally inventory specialization.

In the Fall of 2018, I transferred to San Diego State University to continue my studies. There I studied courses in advanced mathematics, wireless networking fundementals and database implementation, among many others

In the Fall of 2019, I took advantage of an opportunity to work on-campus for the San Diego State University, Instructional Technology Services Department (ITS), after winning a school Hackathon put on by SDSU and ServiceNow. In ITS, I specialized in supporting faculty and staff with using software and tools critical to the education experience. Additionally, I lead efforts by ITS to develop tools and applications that enhance the experience for our students. These projects included designing and implementing a program to convert recorded lecture audio into subtitles and a webtool to assist professors in converting Excel files into compatible CSVs for use in the Parscore grading program.

In the Spring of 2020, I was presented with another opportunity to work with the SDSU IT Division's ServiceNow deployment team. Due to my past experience with the ServiceNow Hackathon, I approched by IT Division leadership to become a member of the campus team bringing ServiceNow to SDSU. Here, my responsibilities ranged from working as an application administrator for our deployment, to a developer, building, testing and maintaining our new service catalog designed to replace the existing Azure system and centralize the University's many on-campus services. Finally, I worked independently within the ServiceNow platform at SDSU to design, build, test and deploy a Mobile IT Service Management applciation for iOS/Android on campus.

Since my graduation in August 2020, I have been actively searching work in application and software development while also broadening my skills and talents by learning new languages, frameworks and tools. I have built this website with the intent to both demonstrate my work beyond what I can put on a resume and also practice, hone and master new skills in web development.
